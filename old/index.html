WORKS

Surreality iOS Game

http://surrealityios.com

Introducing the innovative minimalistic puzzle game - with two hundred hand-crafted unique levels and intriguing, challenging gameplay, players never get bored.
The objective of the game is to launch a wrecking ball to clear all of the on-screen blocks. Every few levels, new obstacles are introduced to raise the bar and keep it fresh.

Development Process

Surreality was created at Make School Summer Academy. Just two weeks was spent on programming the game, since conceptually, it was not a very difficult game to code. The rest of the three weeks of time was spent on designing the game so that the user would not ever get bored.
In the beginning, I had planned on three hundred levels. Naturally, going through these three hundred levels would be extraordinarily tedious, so I had to spend lots of time perfecting the difficulty curve and figuring out when and how I would introduce new concepts.
Additionally, I had to spend time doing app store optimization, cutting out unneeded features and designing the wordless tutorial levels to perfection so that the player would not quit before they had completed the first ten levels.

End Product

At the end, I came up with a stylistic and minimalistic puzzle game with a total of two hundred levels. The art style looks very nice, the levels are done in a way that I am happy with, and everything worked out perfectly. I was awarded the Make School Audience Choice award for my efforts, which made my game one of the top two out of a hundred total apps.

Mindspark Summer

http://mindsparksummer.com

A summer program that introduces new, innovative and different style of teaching that has been shown to work in 2015.

Process

In 2015, I founded a robotics summer program, called Mindspark, dedicated to teaching kids how to work with robotics while making the course as fun as it could possibly be. We started late, in May. While we only got five students, the students thought the program was fantastic.
This year, we are doing it again, but this time we have started far earlier, we are writing hundreds of pages worth of curriculum material, we have built a website from scratch (including a custom built student dashboard), and we have expanded marketing efforts dramatically to gain the students that we need.

Takeaways

The experience from 2015 was the most valuable thing I got from this. I now understand what it feels like to go through the creation of a new business. I have learned valuable lessons, specifically that it is important to not regret mistakes, but to learn from them. The most successful entrepreneurs have failed more than their competitors have tried. This year, with this experience, I plan to make the entire summer program very successful!

Membership Application

bit.ly/membership-app

An innovative way to store your members into an easy-to-use database for future editing, exporting, and printing.

Development Process

This membership application was created with guidelines from a prompt for a Future Business Leaders of America technology competition.
I was already proficient at Java at the time of development for this application. However, to create an application that not only works but also looks great, I had to learn a new Java platform, called JavaFX, which is a platform that replaces Java.swing. I programmed the entire application, and styled it using CSS.

End Product

The application was, at the end, an overall success. It not only looked nice, but the user experience was very well-designed and it is easy to figure out what is going on in the program. This program got first place at a Future Business Leaders of America state-level competition.
