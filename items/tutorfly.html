Overview

Tutorfly is a San Diego-based startup that went through the StartupUCLA accelerator over summer of 2017. From its pilot program, 600+ hours of tutoring have taken place over 150+ tutors. Tutorfly's clients have averaged a 10% increase in final grades; even more have been completed in the company's two-year lifespan. Today, Tutorfly has expanded to 50+ schools with another 700+ online tutors.

As a software engineering intern at this company, I contributed to the code base with half a dozen other college developers. The platform is built with React and Meteor with MongoDB as its backend database, and we used Gitlab as our version control. As a business development intern, I was in charge of various different programs such as the ambassadors program to foster platform growth, I designed marketing materials, and I led the development of the Tutorfly Web Development Course that attracted 45+ students.

Process
Software Engineering Intern

As a software engineer at Tutorfly, I had to work in React and Meteor to develop the web platform and make improvements to it. My tasks included revamping the tutor sorting algorithm to sort by school, restructuring the code for better future efficiency, and designing and implementing the new landing page (pictured above).

Interning here was my first experience with ES6, React, Redux, and Meteor, so picking the framework up was especially challenging. I had to familiarize myself with the codebase as well, which took a long time. Most importantly, I learned collaborative coding, as I had to work together via Gitlab to put the platform together; this will serve as an important skill in future in software engineering jobs.
